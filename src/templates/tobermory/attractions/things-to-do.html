Exploring Tobermory and the Northern Bruce Peninsula

Tucked away on the northern tip of the Bruce Peninsula, Tobermory is a quaint Canadian town home to under 4,000 permanent inhabitants.

Yet the pretty harbourfront village is far from sleepy; its crystal clear waters and centuries-old shipwrecks make Tobermory one of the top freshwater SCUBA destinations on earth. There’s plenty on offer for landlubbers as well, from hiking the forested Bruce Trail to exploring the cragged Grotto cave and Flowerpot Island.

There truly is something for everyone around Tobermory. Let’s take a gander at the top things to do in Tobermory and the Northern Bruce Peninsula.
